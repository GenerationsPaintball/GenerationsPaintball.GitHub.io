What is paintball?

Paintball is a sport in which players compete, in teams or individually, to eliminate opponents by tagging them with gelatin capsules (referred to as paintballs) which contain a water soluble dye. Paintball also requires people to think and strategize quickly and move with strength, speed and agility. Paintballs are propelled from a device called a paintball marker (commonly referred to as a paintball gun). Players learn about teamwork, self-confidence, and develop leadership abilities while having fun and relieving stress.

First Timer Information

Here are some tips for first time paintball players:
  • . Wear something comfortable that can get muddy. Such as a long sleve tee shirt, pants, tennis shoes, and a light jacket.
  • . A hat can be helpful to protect you from the sun and keep the paint out of your hair!
  • . Wear comfortable shoes. You will be on your feet all day so make sure you wear something accordingly. Your shoes will end up very dirty by the end of the day so don't wear something that you want to stay clean.
  • . You can sign your waiver online ahead of time to save some time when you arrive.
  • . Some people like to bring a change of clothing to have clean clothes for the ride home!
  • . Make sure you arrive earlier than you plan on playing, to give you time to sign waivers, get equipment, listen to our safety introduction (rules), and let us answer any questions you may have.
  • . Paintballs may leave slight brusies and may cause slight pain. Because of this we offer a limited number of body vests to reduce the impact. You can also wear extra clothing to prevent brusing and discomfort.
  • . For players that feel that paintball is too painful, there is another option called splatmaster. Splatmaster is a style of paintball, preferably used for children under the age of 8. If you or your child feel that paintball is too painful, this is a great alternitive with the same level of energy and fun.
  • . We play in rain or shine, so do not get discouraged by the weather (thunder or lightning sadly forces us to stop playing).

Field Rules

  • . Markers must remain un-cocked and pointed at the ground with the barrel sock in place at all times when not in a field.
  • . Markers may only be shot within designated fields and when a referee is present.
  • . Goggles and face masks must be worn at all times when in a field.
  • . No physical contact! (no hostages, interrogations, kidnappings, etc.)
  • . Do not shoot at the head or neck, or when the other player is within 10 feet.
  • . No external velocity adjusting devices which would allow the player to alter their markers velocity without disassembely.
  • . No blind shooting. (over cover, around corners, etc.)
  • . No real weapons! (knives, tripwire, firearms, explosives, incendiaries, etc.)
  • . No smoking, no alcohol, and no illegal drugs!
  • . No verbal abuse of any kind. Any remarks about another players race, religion, ethnicity, or parentage will not be tolerated.
  • . No climbing of trees or structures. Do not run into, jump on, jump over, or lean on any field structures.
  • . Do not try to disassemble markers owned by the field, if your equipment needs service a referee or other staff member will gladly assist you.
  • . Avoid field hazards, and be mindful of where you are running!
  • . Do not shoot eliminated players, if a referee sees you shooting at an eliminated player you will be eliminated.
  • . Do not load paintballs from the ground! They will cause your marker to jam and will resualt in misfires and wasted paint.
  • . All paintballs used must be purchased at or apporved by the facility.
  • . No metal cleats or sharp edged shoes.
  • . Referees' decisions are final! Players are not allowed to argue with referees' under any circumstance.
  • . Markers will be set a a velocity of no more than 270 feet per second.
  • . Do not use paintball markers as canes.
  • . Do not shoot into the air, or over a net.
  • . Referees' are not targets, or players in any game so please do not try to shoot them.
The first violation of these rules will resualt in a player being removed from a game, and the second will resualt in the player being unable to play for the rest of the day.
